# OPSEC MODEL

## 1 Purpose

Operational security is the practice of controlling what information can be observed, collected, and linked by an external observer.

The goal is not total invisibility. The goal is controlled exposure.

---

## 2 Core Principle

Security is based on separation, minimization, and unpredictability.

If data points cannot be connected, identity cannot be reconstructed.

---

## 3 Fundamental Layers

1 Identity layer  
Defines who you are in a system

2 Behavior layer  
Defines how you act and interact

3 Network layer  
Defines how your traffic and connections appear

4 Data layer  
Defines what files and information you produce

---

## 4 Security Objective

The objective is to prevent correlation across layers.

Even if one layer is exposed, the others must remain isolated.

---

## 5 Core Rule

Never allow two separate operational contexts to share reusable signals such as patterns, identifiers, or behavior traces.

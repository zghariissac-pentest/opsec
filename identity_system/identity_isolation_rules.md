# IDENTITY ISOLATION RULES

## 1 Core Principle

Each identity must exist as a fully independent operational environment.

No shared signals are allowed between identities.

---

## 2 Separation Rules

1 No shared usernames across systems  
2 No shared email patterns  
3 No repeated behavioral timing patterns  
4 No reused writing style in sensitive contexts  

---

## 3 Network Separation

Each identity must use independent routing behavior and session structure.

Cross usage is considered compromise.

---

## 4 Data Separation

Files, logs, and metadata must never be shared across identities.

Each identity produces isolated outputs only.

---

## 5 Behavioral Control

Avoid repeating actions that create patterns.

Patterns are the strongest form of identification.

---

## 6 Final Rule

If two identities can be linked by behavior, metadata, or timing then separation has failed.
